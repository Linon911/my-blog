## Why Essential

Maskus provides the first systematic empirical analysis of how IPR strength affects technology flows. His finding that licensing is more sensitive to IPR than FDI remains a cornerstone of the literature.

## Key Results

- Stronger IPRs increase licensing revenues from affiliates and unaffiliated firms.
- The effect is non-linear: too-weak *and* too-strong protection can deter transfer.
- Middle-income countries face the starkest trade-off.
