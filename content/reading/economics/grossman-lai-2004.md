## Summary

Grossman & Lai (2004, AER) develop a two-country model of optimal patent policy when innovation occurs in both the North and South. Their key result is that harmonisation at the Northern optimum is not globally efficient — the South prefers weaker protection.

## Relevance

This is the direct theoretical predecessor of my working paper on optimal patent length in open economies.
