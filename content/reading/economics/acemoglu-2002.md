Acemoglu, Johnson, and Robinson (2001) provide the foundational empirical framework for understanding how institutions — rather than geography or culture alone — drive long-run economic development. Using settler mortality as an instrument for institutional quality, they demonstrate that property rights protection has a large causal effect on per capita income.
