I am a researcher working at the intersection of international trade, intellectual property law, and economic theory. My work asks how legal institutions shape the incentives of firms and governments in a globally integrated economy.

I am particularly interested in **technology transfer** — the mechanisms by which knowledge and innovation diffuse across borders — and how different intellectual property regimes facilitate or impede this process. My current research uses gravity models with PPML estimation and exploits variation in IP harmonization (induced by TRIPS and TRIPS-plus agreements) to identify causal effects on bilateral technology flows.

Methodologically, I combine contract-theoretic modeling with empirical work using panel data and quasi-experimental designs. I believe that good economic research must speak to both the theory and the data — neither pure formalism nor pure empiricism is sufficient for understanding complex institutional phenomena.
