## Overview

Technology transfer — the cross-border movement of knowledge, processes, and innovations — is central to economic development. My research asks how the contractual structure of technology licensing, the strength of IP protection in recipient countries, and macroeconomic environments jointly determine the volume and character of technology flows.

## Key Questions

- Under what conditions do technology-owning firms prefer licensing over FDI?
- How does IP regime uncertainty affect contractual terms?
- What are the macroeconomic spillovers in middle-income countries?

## Formal Setup

Let $V$ be the value of technology and $\phi \in [0,1]$ IP protection strength. The licensor solves:

$$\max_{R,\,s} \; R + s \cdot \phi V \quad \text{s.t.} \; R + s \cdot \phi V \geq F$$

where $R$ is the fixed royalty, $s$ is the revenue-share rate, and $F$ is the transfer cost.
