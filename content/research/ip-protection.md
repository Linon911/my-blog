## Overview

I study how the design of intellectual property systems interacts with market structure and innovation incentives. A core interest is optimal IP policy in open economies with heterogeneous trading partners.

## Patent Breadth Trade-off

Broader patents increase innovation incentives but impose larger static welfare losses. The optimal breadth $b^*$ solves:

$$\frac{\partial \pi(b)}{\partial b} \cdot I'(\pi) = \frac{\partial DWL(b)}{\partial b}$$

where $I(\pi)$ is the innovation function and $DWL(b)$ is deadweight loss as a function of breadth.
